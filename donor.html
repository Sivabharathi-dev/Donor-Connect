Packed Red Blood Cells (PRBCs)

What is it?

Blood collected directly from the donor into a blood bag and mixed with an anticoagulant is known as whole blood. This whole blood is then centrifuged to separate red cells, platelets, and plasma. The separated red cells are mixed with a preservative and are referred to as packed red blood cells (PRBCs).

Who can donate?

  • Age: 18-65 years
  • Weight: 45 kg or more
  • Health: Fit and healthy

Used For

Correction of severe anemia in various conditions, treating blood loss due to childbirth, surgery, or trauma.

Storage Duration

PRBCs can be stored for up to 42 days at 2-6Â°C.

Donation Time

It takes about 15-30 minutes to donate, including the pre-donation check-up.

Donation Frequency

Male donors can donate again after 90 days. Female donors can donate again after 120 days.
